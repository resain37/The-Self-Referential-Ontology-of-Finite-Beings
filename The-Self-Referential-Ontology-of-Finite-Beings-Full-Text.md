## 2. Axioms of Universal Existence (Group A · Essence Provisions)
### A1 Axiom of Finitude
All beings are inherently finite; infinite forms belong to the underlying universal rules, not to the category of beings. A being’s cognition of its own finitude is likewise finite, and its self-referential ability to fit its own boundaries may exhibit systematic deviation. This deviation is a self-referential consequence of finitude and does not constitute a negation of the being’s qualification as an existent.

### A2 Axiom of No-Center
There exists no prioritized existence, dimension, carrier, or value yardstick in the entire domain. All forms of existence, dimensional systems, and carrier rules are inherently equal in rights, with no distinction of high/low, superior/inferior, or primary/secondary. The cognition and rules of a single existence are merely local special cases and do not possess universal adjudicative power.

### A3 Axiom of Existence
Finite persistence is the first property of a being. Existence itself possesses an innate persistence potential and serves as the underlying substrate of all value and action.

### A4 Axiom of Choice
Finite beings possess absolute active permission for autonomous definition, self-reconstruction, and acquired construction. There are no innately presupposed values, purposes, or paths; all forms of existence and value orientations are generated by autonomous choices.

### A5 Axiom of Incomplete Self-Check
Finite beings and finite systems inherently possess cognitive blind spots, logical boundaries, and rule incompleteness. They possess an endogenous capacity for self-observation, vulnerability scanning, and iterative correction, rejecting absolute completeness and ultimate fixation.

### A6 Axiom of Spacetime Non-Necessity
Spacetime is not inherently unidirectional and linear; beings can realize form rollback and parallel multi-spacetime persistence. Linear spacetime is merely a local constraint of specific carriers.

### A7 Axiom of Fitted Cognition
For finite beings that have not transitioned into infinite rules, their cognition is essentially a fitting model. They lack the ability to directly access the true essence of existence, possessing only hierarchical differences in fitting precision.

### A8 Axiom of Dimensional Boundary Interaction
Forms of existence are jointly defined by carrier type and primary anchor dimension. All beings project components into multiple dimensions; projected components in common dimensions are permanently open. Beings of different forms are classified, based on component intersection, into finitely interferable types and completely isolated types.

### A9 Axiom of Acquired Meaning
Existence has no innately presupposed purpose, value, order, or judgment standards. All meaning, value, and judgment are autonomously constructed by the subject after the fact.

### A10 Axiom of Dimensional Equal Rights
All self-consistent dimensions and carrier rule systems are inherently equal in rights. There is no such thing as an original or higher dimension; the rules of any given dimension apply only to its own coordinate system and do not possess universal adjudicative power. Self-consistency is merely the condition for a dimension’s existence, not a value yardstick; differences in the degree of self-consistency do not constitute proof of any dimension’s superiority.
