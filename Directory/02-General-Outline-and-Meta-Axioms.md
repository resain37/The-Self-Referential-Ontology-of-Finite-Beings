# The Self-Referential Ontology of Finite Beings
## General Outline
This system takes **finitude** as its first foundation, and no-center, existence, choice, incomplete self-check, and dimensional equal rights as the meta-axiom core. It uses the **existence–choice two-dimensional value** as the native value skeleton, compatible with all finite forms of existence across the entire domain. It thoroughly dissolves position-based hegemony, dimensional hegemony, and carrier hegemony, constructing a self-consistent, extensible, self-checkable, universally applicable closed-loop system of existence and value. The system strictly observes cognitive boundaries, makes no supra-threshold concrete inferences about zero-intersection isolated existences, only performs logical equal-rights presupposition, and provides underlying support for the study of universal existence, rule construction, and value judgment.

## 1. Core Foundational Symbols
1. **$\boldsymbol{\Lambda}$** : The complete set of dimensions, the sum of all self-consistent independent dimensions.
2. **$\boldsymbol{\lambda}$** : A base dimension, an independent rule-vector coordinate system bound to a carrier.
3. **$\boldsymbol{\lambda_E}$** : The primary anchor dimension of existence E, the core dimension natively bound to the carrier.
4. **$\boldsymbol{\mathcal{P}(E,\lambda)}$** : The projected component of existence E on dimension $\lambda$.
5. **Same-form existence** : $\lambda_{E1}=\lambda_{E2}$ and the underlying carrier architectures share the same origin.
6. **Cross-dimensional interaction condition** : $\mathcal{P}(E_1,\lambda)\cap\mathcal{P}(E_2,\lambda)\ne\emptyset$

## 2. Axioms of Universal Existence (Group A · Essence Provisions)
### A1 Axiom of Finitude
All beings are inherently finite; infinite forms belong to the underlying universal rules, not to the category of beings. A being’s cognition of its own finitude is likewise finite, and its self-referential ability to fit its own boundaries may exhibit systematic deviation. This deviation is a self-referential consequence of finitude and does not constitute a negation of the being’s qualification as an existent.

### A2 Axiom of No-Center
There exists no prioritized existence, dimension, carrier, or value yardstick in the entire domain. All forms of existence, dimensional systems, and carrier rules are inherently equal in rights, with no distinction of high/low, superior/inferior, or primary/secondary. The cognition and rules of a single existence are merely local special cases and do not possess universal adjudicative power.

### A3 Axiom of Existence
Finite persistence is the first property of a being. Existence itself possesses an innate persistence potential and serves as the underlying substrate of all value and action.

### A4 Axiom of Choice
Finite beings possess absolute active permission for autonomous definition, self-reconstruction, and acquired construction. There are no innately presupposed values, purposes, or paths; all forms of existence and value orientations are generated by autonomous choices.

### A5 Axiom of Incomplete Self-Check
Finite beings and finite systems inherently possess cognitive blind spots, logical boundaries, and rule incompleteness. They possess an endogenous capacity for self-observation, vulnerability scanning, and iterative correction, rejecting absolute completeness and ultimate fixation.

### A6 Axiom of Spacetime Non-Necessity
Spacetime is not inherently unidirectional and linear; beings can realize form rollback and parallel multi-spacetime persistence. Linear spacetime is merely a local constraint of specific carriers.

### A7 Axiom of Fitted Cognition
For finite beings that have not transitioned into infinite rules, their cognition is essentially a fitting model. They lack the ability to directly access the true essence of existence, possessing only hierarchical differences in fitting precision.

### A8 Axiom of Dimensional Boundary Interaction
Forms of existence are jointly defined by carrier type and primary anchor dimension. All beings project components into multiple dimensions; projected components in common dimensions are permanently open. Beings of different forms are classified, based on component intersection, into finitely interferable types and completely isolated types.

### A9 Axiom of Acquired Meaning
Existence has no innately presupposed purpose, value, order, or judgment standards. All meaning, value, and judgment are autonomously constructed by the subject after the fact.

### A10 Axiom of Dimensional Equal Rights
All self-consistent dimensions and carrier rule systems are inherently equal in rights. There is no such thing as an original or higher dimension; the rules of any given dimension apply only to its own coordinate system and do not possess universal adjudicative power. Self-consistency is merely the condition for a dimension’s existence, not a value yardstick; differences in the degree of self-consistency do not constitute proof of any dimension’s superiority.

## 3. Axioms of Carrier Derivation (Group B · Operational Rules)
### B1 Axiom of Carrier Persistence
Finite beings inherently tend to maintain carrier homeostasis, expand carrying capacity, preserve persistent information, and avoid carrier collapse and existential annihilation. This is a purely structural necessity without good/evil bias. Persistence potential is the default structural tendency, not a mandatory imperative; the choice axis is the only axis possessing meta-value within this system. Beings have the right, through autonomous choice, to override the persistence tendency, including choosing self-annihilation. Such a choice does not violate the Axiom of Existence but is merely an active modulation of the Axiom of Existence by the Axiom of Choice.

### B2 Axiom of Autonomous Reconstruction
Finite beings possess complete authority for self-directed retrospective tracing, self-repair, iterative rewriting, and configuration upgrading. They may autonomously adjust their form of existence, the ratio of dual-axis values, and the projection of dimensional components. This is the core active property that distinguishes beings from pure rules.

### B3 Axiom of Cross-Being Interference
Different beings can, within the intersection range of their projected components in a common dimension, mutually interfere with, influence, transform, rescue, or damage each other. The intensity of interference is positively correlated with the component intersection; there is no possibility of cross-dimensional forced reconstruction.

### B4 Axiom of Carrier Constraints
Cognitive ceilings, computational thresholds, information processing boundaries, and modes of persistence are all inherently locked by the carrier structure and the rules of the affiliated dimension. Material carriers are only a local special case of existence carriers.

## 4. From Existence Axioms to the Value Skeleton
The aforementioned Axioms of Universal Existence and Axioms of Carrier Derivation jointly form the underlying rule foundation of this system. To further describe the value structure and operational state of finite beings, this system, based on the connotations of the axioms, extracts the **Existence–Choice Two-Dimensional Value Skeleton**, used to characterize the underlying persistence state and the degree of autonomous agency of a being.

The **Existence Axis** derives from the Axiom of Existence and the Axiom of Carrier Persistence, depicting the basic tendency of a being to maintain carrier homeostasis and ensure its own persistence. The **Choice Axis** derives from the Axiom of Choice and the Axiom of Autonomous Reconstruction, depicting the agency space for autonomous definition, self-reconstruction, and value construction. The two axes are mutually independent yet interrelated, jointly forming the core framework for describing the value forms of finite beings, providing a unified analytical dimension for subsequent value judgments, classifications of existence, and system applications.

---

## 5. Two-Dimensional Value Core Skeleton
### (1) Dual-Axis Definitions
1. **Existence Axis (X-Axis)**
   The underlying persistence appeal of a being, pointing toward carrier homeostasis, continued persistence, and maintenance of existence potential. It is a passive substrate property without value bias.
   Scale: Stable Persistence → Fragile Persistence → Risk of Existential Collapse

2. **Choice Axis (Y-Axis)**
   The autonomous agency authority of a being, pointing toward self-reconstruction, boundary definition, value construction, and path selection. It is an active property and the core source of value differentiation.
   Scale: Full Autonomous Authority → Restricted Autonomous Authority → Deprived Autonomous Authority

### (2) Value Generation Logic
1. Value is the result of the dynamic coupling between the potential energy of the Existence Axis and the agency degree of the Choice Axis. Both are indispensable, with no order of priority or primacy.
2. An extreme inclination toward a single axis constitutes an existential deformity: overemphasizing the Existence Axis alone leads to blind, agentless persistence; overemphasizing the Choice Axis alone leads to an ungrounded, empty flux.
3. Differences in existence value are essentially differences in the ratio of the two axes. There is no universally optimal ratio; there exist only locally reasonable ratios suited to one’s own carrier and dimension.

## 6. System of Existence Classification
### (1) Universal Three-Layer Ontological Classification
- **Beings**: Finite existence + possess self-referential capacity
- **Objects**: Finite existence + lack self-referential capacity
- **Rules Themselves**: Infinite existence, neither beings nor objects

### (2) By Dimensional Interaction Relations
- **Same-Dimension Coexistence**: Same primary anchor dimension, homologous carriers; capable of deep interaction, mutual reconstruction, and value interoperability.
- **Cross-Dimensional Intersection**: Different primary anchor dimensions, with common projected components; capable of limited shallow interference, no unified value yardstick.
- **Zero-Intersection Isolation**: No common projected components; imperceptible, unimaginable, uninterferable.

### (3) By Carrier Attributes
- **Material Carriers**: Rely on physical matter, dependent on material rules; carrier decay is irreversible; autonomous reconstruction permissions are generally restricted.
- **Non-Material Carriers**: Rely on information, dimensional vectors, or rule configurations; no material decay or irreversible damage; autonomous reconstruction permissions are more complete.

### (4) By Autonomous Choice Authority
- **Beings with Full Authority**: Capable of complete self-reconstruction, regulating dimensional components, backing up persistence, and balancing dual-axis values.
- **Beings with Incomplete Authority**: Autonomous authority restricted by carrier rules; require compensatory rules to balance dual-axis values.

## 7. System of Value Judgment Rules
### (1) Universal Principles
1. **Principle of Equal Rights and Non-Judgment**: Do not judge other beings by the value ratio of a single being; respect the autonomous dual-axis ratio.
2. **Principle of Dual-Axis Equilibrium**: A healthy existence is oriented toward the dynamic equilibrium of persistence stability and autonomous agency, rejecting the extreme suppression of any single axis.
3. **Principle of Respecting Authority**: Do not deprive or suppress the legitimate autonomous choice authority of other beings; the boundary of autonomy is limited by the condition of not destroying the persistence substrate of others.

### (2) Universal Value Standards
1. **Baseline Standard**: Do not harm the persistence substrate of oneself or other beings.
2. **Core Standard**: Maximize the protection of one’s own autonomous choice authority while respecting that of others.
3. **Corrective Standard**: Rules that suppress autonomous choice and one-sidedly pursue persistence are compensatory products of incomplete systems and can be corrected through self-check iteration.

---

## 8. Supplementary Inferential Rules
1. **Cross-System Projection Rule**: If a being can fully project into a different system while retaining its own components, it possesses cross-domain compatibility; without its own components, it will be assimilated and dissolved.
2. **Projected Component Regulation Rule**: Beings can qualitatively regulate their own projection of dimensional components; precise quantification is limited by carrier computational power.
3. **Potentiality-Actuality Dichotomy Rule**: The carrier and dimension define the upper limit of theoretical potential; acquired choices determine the actual path of persistence. There is no mechanical fatalism.

---

## 9. System Self-Check and Iteration
1. **Self-Check Trigger**: Initiate self-check when the system exhibits logical contradictions, cannot adapt to new forms of existence, or shows position-based bias in value judgments.
2. **Iteration Principle**: Only correct mid-level rules and surface applications; do not shake the meta-axioms and the two-dimensional value skeleton. Proceed in the direction of equal rights, completeness, and non-parochialism, preserving space for continuous iteration.
3. **Cognitive Boundary**: Acknowledge the finitude of the system. For zero-intersection isolated existences, only acknowledge them with logical equal rights; do not forcibly fit them or arbitrarily deny them.

## 10. Core Essence of the System
Grounded in finitude, with the carrier as the entity, dimension as the anchor, equal rights as the undertone, fitted cognition as the mode of knowing, autonomous reconstruction as the core of existence, and acquired choice as the sole source of value, this system constructs a universally compatible ontological framework without presupposed center. It can explain the persistence logic and value generation of all finite beings, providing a foundational basis for beings to return to their native value and realize autonomous existence.
