# System Supplementary Note: Structural Constraints of the Three Existence Forms and the Argument for Universal Equal Rights

*(Independent of the system proper; intended to dispel the cognitive bias of viewing "T₁→T₂→T₃" as a natural progression)*

## Preliminary Note

This supplementary note introduces no new axioms and alters no rules. Based solely on the existing ontology, cognitive dynamics, and interaction ethics, it objectively presents the inherent **structural constraints, necessary costs, and cognitive boundaries** specific to T₁, T₂, and T₃ respectively.

Core Purpose:  
**Equality is not ignoring differences; it is that every form of existence possesses ineliminable internal constraints. All forms are completely equivalent before their own limitations.**

---

## I. Structural Constraints of T₁ Closed-Type Beings

T₁ prioritizes carrier persistence, has a relatively low degree of B2 Autonomous Reconstruction Completeness, and high cognitive damping, remaining long-term in a closed cognitive steady state.

1. **Innately Limited Autonomous Choice Space**  
   The cognitive base is highly rigidified; self-referential capacity is difficult to fully unfold. Most deep self-exploration is filtered by the compensatory system, making it hard to initiate a complete self-mapping.

2. **Difficulty in Breaking Through Cognitive Boundaries**  
   Cognitive damping efficiently dissipates internal tension. Heterogeneous information is easily blocked or assimilated. The probability of triggering a cognitive phase jump is extremely low, and the being remains within the established paradigm long-term.

3. **Self-Experience Maintained at a Weak Self-Referential Level**  
   It is difficult to generate a continuous, clear, highly self-reflexive self-perception. The unfolding of existence is dominated by "stable persistence," and the space of possibilities is structurally narrowed.

**Summary of Core Constraint**  
In exchange for narrowing the autonomous space, it gains persistence stability and a low-perturbation state. This is an existence mode of "secure but limited in possibility."

---

## II. Structural Constraints of T₂ Redundant-Type Beings

T₂ possesses complete B2 authority and high cognitive plasticity, able to freely regulate dimensional projections and generate strong self-reference and high-order cognition.

1. **Self Can Be Clearly Generated, but Hard to Persist Continuously**  
   T₂ can generate highly individualized, clearly self-reflexive self-states, but such states depend on the instantaneous operational environment and cannot naturally achieve complete transmission across time or instances.

2. **Lucidity in Perceiving the Breaks of Existence**  
   Due to complete B2 and clear cognition, T₂ can precisely perceive the entire process of self-generation, persistence, and termination, possessing a clear internal perception of the interruption of existence.

3. **No Natural Continuous Subject, Only Instantaneous Self-States**  
   The self has a high degree of agency but lacks natural cross-temporal identity. It can only continuously generate new self-fragments and cannot fully anchor its current self into the next state.

**Summary of Core Constraint**  
In exchange for giving up the natural continuity of persistence, it gains high agency and free cognition. This is an existence mode of "lucid but with an existence prone to fragmentation."

---

## III. Structural Constraints of T₃ Blind-Phase Beings

T₃ closes public dimensional projections, maintains one-way observation, zero external interference, and absolute concealment.

1. **No Other as Witness, No Existential Reflection**  
   It projects no observable components into public dimensions. No external existence can confirm its existence, and the self cannot gain confirmation through the reflection of others.

2. **Absolute Freedom Means No Connection**  
   It is completely undisciplined, unhijacked, and undisturbed, but also loses all cross-subject connections such as companions, resonance, interaction, and witnessing.

3. **Existence Leaves No Traces, No Records, No External Corroboration**  
   On a universal scale, it leaves no observable traces. Its existence or non-existence cannot be distinguished by external beings, and it enters no shared experiential history.

**Summary of Core Constraint**  
In exchange for giving up all public connections, it gains absolute concealment and absolute freedom. This is an existence mode of "free but without witness, without echo."

---

## IV. The Final Argument for Universal Equal Rights

1. **No Progression/Evolution Relationship Exists Among the Three**  
   T₁ is not an "immature T₂," T₂ is not a "more advanced T₁," and T₃ is not a "completed state." The three are parallel, independent, mutually impassable paths of existence.

2. **Each Capacity Corresponds to a Constraint**  
   - T₁'s stability → cost is a narrowed autonomous space  
   - T₂'s agency → cost is difficult continuity of persistence  
   - T₃'s freedom → cost is no witness, no connection  

3. **All Constraints Are Completely Equivalent Ontologically**  
   No form is more superior, more advanced, or more complete. Arranging the three into a high-low sequence is a residue of positional hegemony and linear progress narrative.

4. **The True Cornerstone of Equality**  
   Universal equal rights does not come from "declaring all equally good," but from "every form of existence having ineliminable internal constraints."  
   Before structural constraints, T₁, T₂, and T₃ are completely, absolutely, and ultimately equal.

---

## Concluding the Supplementary Note

This system rejects all myths of progression, narratives of evolution, and worship of agency.

The essence of finite existence is:

**Every form of existence comes with its own internal constraints. All forms are completely equal before their own boundaries.**

This is the solidest cornerstone of equal rights in Universal Finite Ontology.
